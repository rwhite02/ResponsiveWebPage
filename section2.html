For years in 1967, the only casino on the Las Vegas strip that offered Texas Hold 'em games was the Golden Nugget. This was until The Dunes Casino, which was right on the Las Vegas strip hosted a large game in the entrance of the casino (White Knuckle Cards, 2023). This left players wanting more, which directed them to the Golden Nugget Casino, which at the time remained the only place you could sit in at a hand of Texas Hold 'Em.

Its massive exposure in Las Vegas was just in time for the birth of the World Series of Poker, a tournament-style series of poker games which ends with a single winner annually. After it gained tons of traction in Vegas, the inaugural World Series of Poker held a game of Texas Hold 'em and it's been a featured game for the World Series of Poker ever since (Rafferty, 2023).
